Lyft Pick Up Data Analysis - NYC


Welcome to our project!

We're all familiar with LYFT, NYC's second biggest ride share company. Though it IPO'd at a valuation of $23B last year, it was valued at measly $2B in 2014. We decided to analyze Lyft pick up data in NYC and outer boroughs at a point in time in 2014. Our data consisted of 5,748 uniquely distinct pick-ups made spanning from 12AM to 11:59PM on September 5, 2014.

In order to analyze our data, we created a RESTful API using the CSV file with Flask. Then we used HTML, Javascript, Plotly and D3 to come up with cool maps and graphics.

To map all the coordinates, we made a map with all the pick-up locations represented by markers. Since there were so many locations, we created a cluster map to make it quick and easy to spot how many pick-ups were made at a given area. Finally, we created a heatmap to show (quite literally) pick up hot spots througout New York City.

In terms of data analysis, we decided to check the frequency of pick-ups by the hour and every three hours to see what times people are using Lyft more frequently.
